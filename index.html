Why does food matter so much?

What you eat can either support your healing or silently make things worse. This guide is written for real people — not doctors — so you'll find clear, simple advice for some of the most common health conditions. Always use this as a starting point, and talk to your doctor or nutritionist for a plan that's just right for you. 💚

Metabolic Conditions
🩸

Diabetes

Diabetes affects how your body uses sugar (glucose). When you eat, your blood sugar rises — and with diabetes, it rises too high. The right foods help keep your blood sugar steady and your energy balanced all day.

✅ Eat More Of

  • Leafy greens (spinach, ugwu, kale)
  • Whole grains (oats, brown rice, millet)
  • Beans, lentils, and chickpeas
  • Fatty fish like mackerel and salmon
  • Nuts and seeds (in small amounts)
  • Avocados and berries
  • Unripe plantain and sweet potatoes

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Sugary drinks (sodas, juice, energy drinks)
  • White bread, white rice (in large portions)
  • Fried and processed foods
  • Cakes, biscuits, and pastries
  • Very ripe bananas and overripe plantain
  • Alcohol — it confuses blood sugar levels

🫃

Stomach Ulcer

A stomach ulcer is a sore on the lining of your stomach or small intestine. Certain foods irritate that sore and worsen pain — while others soothe and gently help it heal.

✅ Eat More Of

  • Cooked vegetables (carrots, broccoli, cabbage)
  • Unripe plantain — a natural stomach soother
  • Oatmeal and soft porridges
  • Bananas (they coat the stomach wall)
  • Boiled chicken or fish (lean proteins)
  • Honey — has natural anti-bacterial properties
  • Yoghurt with probiotics

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Spicy foods — pepper, chilli, hot sauce
  • Alcohol — eats away at the stomach lining
  • Coffee and strong tea (increase acid)
  • Fried and oily foods
  • Citrus fruits like oranges and lemons
  • Carbonated drinks (fizzy sodas)

Doctor's note: Never skip meals with an ulcer — an empty stomach produces acid that attacks the wound. Eat small meals every 3–4 hours. Most ulcers are caused by a bacteria called H. pylori and can be treated with medication.

🥛

Lactose Intolerance

People with lactose intolerance can't fully digest lactose — the sugar found in milk and dairy. It causes bloating, cramps, and diarrhea when dairy is consumed.

✅ Eat More Of

  • Plant-based milks (soy, oat, almond, coconut)
  • Hard cheeses like cheddar (lower in lactose)
  • Lactose-free yoghurt and milk
  • Calcium-rich vegetables: kale, broccoli, ugu
  • Sardines and other fish with soft bones
  • Tofu and soy products

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Regular cow's milk
  • Ice cream and milkshakes
  • Soft cheeses (cream cheese, cottage cheese)
  • Butter and cream-based sauces
  • Processed foods with hidden milk (check labels)
  • Dairy-based desserts and chocolates

Neurological & Movement Conditions
🧠

Cerebral Palsy

Cerebral palsy affects movement and muscle control. Many people with CP have difficulty chewing or swallowing, and may need extra calories due to higher muscle activity. Good nutrition supports strength, brain health, and quality of life.

✅ Eat More Of

  • Soft, easy-to-chew foods (mashed yam, pap, custard)
  • Calorie-dense foods (nut butters, avocado)
  • Omega-3 rich foods (fish, flaxseed) for brain health
  • Iron-rich foods (liver, beans, green vegetables)
  • High-fibre foods to prevent constipation
  • Smoothies and fortified drinks for easy nutrition

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Hard, crunchy foods — difficult to chew safely
  • Stringy or chewy meats — choking risk
  • Thin watery liquids — thicken them instead
  • Sticky foods like peanut candy or gummy sweets
  • High-sugar foods that cause energy crashes
  • Junk food with little nutritional value

Cardiovascular Health
❤️

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Hypertension means your blood is pushing too hard against your artery walls. Over time, this strains your heart and kidneys. Salt and potassium intake have a massive impact on your blood pressure levels.

✅ Eat More Of

  • Potassium-rich foods (bananas, potatoes, spinach)
  • Oats and whole grains
  • Garlic — naturally lowers blood pressure
  • Low-fat dairy (yoghurt, low-fat milk)
  • Fatty fish (mackerel, salmon)
  • Berries and beets
  • Unsalted nuts and seeds

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Table salt and salty seasonings (Maggi, Knorr)
  • Processed and canned foods (hidden salt)
  • Red meat in large quantities
  • Alcohol — raises blood pressure significantly
  • Fried and fast foods
  • Pickled foods and salty snacks

⚖️

Obesity, High Cholesterol & Heart Disease

These three conditions are deeply linked. Excess body weight often raises cholesterol levels, which builds up as plaque in your arteries — narrowing them over time. This can lead to coronary artery blockage, chest pain, heart attack, and death. The good news? Food and lifestyle changes can break this chain.

⚠️ How It Escalates — The Danger Chain

Obesity
→
High Cholesterol (LDL)
→
Artery Plaque Buildup
→
Coronary Artery Blockage
→
Heart Attack / Death

✅ Eat More Of

  • Oats and barley — lower LDL (bad cholesterol)
  • Fatty fish (omega-3 reduces inflammation)
  • Olive oil instead of vegetable or palm oil
  • Avocados — full of heart-healthy fats
  • Fruits and vegetables high in fibre
  • Nuts (almonds, walnuts) in small portions
  • Legumes — beans, lentils, soybeans
  • Dark chocolate (70%+ cocoa, small amounts)
  • Green tea — helps reduce cholesterol

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Trans fats — fried foods, margarine, pastries
  • Saturated fats — fatty meats, full-fat dairy
  • Sugary drinks and snacks — convert to fat
  • Fast food and processed meat (sausages, hotdogs)
  • Refined carbs — white bread, white rice in excess
  • Palm oil and coconut oil in large amounts
  • Alcohol — adds empty calories, raises triglycerides
  • Late-night heavy meals — slow digestion stores as fat

🏃 Practices to Help Lose Weight & Protect Your Heart

🚶

Walk 30 Minutes Daily Even a brisk walk after meals burns calories and improves heart health significantly.

🍽️

Eat Smaller Portions Use a smaller plate. Stop eating when you're 80% full — your stomach takes 20 mins to signal fullness.

💧

Drink Water Before Meals Drinking 500ml of water 30 minutes before meals naturally reduces how much you eat.

😴

Sleep 7–8 Hours Poor sleep increases hunger hormones, making you crave fatty and sugary foods more.

🧘

Manage Stress Stress triggers cortisol, a hormone that causes fat to store around your belly — the most dangerous area.

🚫

Avoid Late-Night Eating Stop eating 2–3 hours before bedtime. Your body stores food as fat when you're inactive.

👨‍⚕️

Doctor's note: Aim to lose weight gradually — 0.5 to 1kg per week is healthy and sustainable. Crash diets don't work long-term. Ask your doctor for a cholesterol check (lipid panel test) at least once a year. Statins may be prescribed if your cholesterol is dangerously high.

Bone & Joint Health
🦴

Arthritis (Weak Bones & Joint Pain)

Arthritis causes pain, swelling, and stiffness in your joints. It can also weaken the cartilage — the soft cushion between your bones. There are two main types: Osteoarthritis (wear and tear with age) and Rheumatoid Arthritis (the immune system attacks joints). Diet and lifestyle can significantly reduce pain and slow damage.

✅ Eat More Of

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel) — reduce joint inflammation
  • Calcium-rich foods for bone strength (dairy, kale, ugu)
  • Vitamin D sources (eggs, fortified milk, sunlight)
  • Turmeric and ginger — natural anti-inflammatory spices
  • Broccoli — contains sulforaphane, protects cartilage
  • Berries — rich in antioxidants that fight inflammation
  • Olive oil — acts like a natural anti-inflammatory drug
  • Bone broth — rebuilds cartilage (collagen-rich)

❌ Try to Avoid

  • Red meat and processed meats — trigger inflammation
  • Fried and fast foods — increase inflammatory markers
  • Sugar and sugary drinks — worsen joint swelling
  • Refined carbohydrates (white bread, pastries)
  • Alcohol — interferes with arthritis medications
  • Excess salt — causes water retention, worsens swelling
  • Dairy in large amounts (for some people)

☀️

Get Sunlight Daily

10–15 minutes of morning sun gives your body Vitamin D, which is essential for absorbing calcium and keeping bones strong.

🏊

Try Low-Impact Exercise

Swimming, yoga, and walking put less pressure on joints while keeping them mobile and muscles strong around them.

🌡️

Hot & Cold Therapy

Warm compresses reduce stiffness. Cold packs reduce swelling. Alternate them for 15–20 minutes during flare-ups.

💊

Glucosamine & Collagen

Supplements like glucosamine and collagen peptides help rebuild cartilage. Ask your doctor before starting any supplement.

⚖️

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Every extra kilogram puts 4kg of pressure on your knee joints. Losing even 5kg can dramatically reduce pain.

🧘

Practice Good Posture

Poor posture puts uneven stress on joints. Sitting upright and sleeping in a supportive position protects your spine and knees.

👨‍⚕️

Doctor's note: Arthritis has no cure, but it can be managed well with the right diet, movement, and medication. Don't wait until joints are severely damaged before seeking help. Early intervention makes a big difference. A physiotherapist can design a safe exercise program just for your condition.

Food Allergies

⚠️ Common Food Allergies

Food allergies are different from food intolerances. With an allergy, even a tiny amount of the food can trigger a serious — sometimes life-threatening — reaction. Here are the most common ones to know:

🥜

Peanut Allergy

One of the most serious. Reactions range from skin rash to anaphylaxis (throat swelling). Avoid all peanut products including peanut butter and groundnut oil.

🌾

Gluten / Celiac Disease

Gluten (in wheat, barley, rye) damages the gut lining for people with celiac. Choose rice, cassava, sorghum, and certified gluten-free products.

🦐

Shellfish Allergy

Shrimp, crayfish, crab, and lobster can cause severe reactions. Even cooking steam can trigger responses in sensitive people.

🥚

Egg Allergy

More common in children. Watch for eggs hidden in baked goods, pasta, and sauces. Many children grow out of this allergy over time.

🐟

Fish Allergy

Different from shellfish allergy. Common fish triggers include tuna, catfish, and cod. Always check food labels for fish-derived ingredients.

🌰

Tree Nut Allergy

Includes cashews, walnuts, almonds. Often causes severe reactions. Carry an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if diagnosed.
